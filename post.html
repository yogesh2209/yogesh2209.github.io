The Journey

It has been a roller-coaster ride but thorougly enjoyed the project. The journey is divided into following steps.

The Idea

The Idea was born out of curiosity of the nowadays technology of autonomous cars, drones, and robots in general.

We can do it

In order to tackle a project of this magnitude, we needed skills in hardware, software, and analytics. Thankfully each integrant had those qualities, so we were on our way.

Putting it together

Putting the car together was the most challenging part of the project, it all started with trying to hack into the remote control of the car using the Arduino Uno. The controller came with a variable resistor (potentiometer) that controlled the speed of the car. Then we move on to a second car, one with s simpler controller. Turn out we needed a relay to control the switches. We ended up hacking into the motor of the car instead using the raspberry pi and a motor driver module.

Running the software

After installing the peripheral accessories like camera, and distance sensor, we proceeded with the installation of the software into the raspberry pi.

Testing and Results

We tested the car successfully, it was able to avoid a front obstacle and stop for a red traffic light and a stop sign.
